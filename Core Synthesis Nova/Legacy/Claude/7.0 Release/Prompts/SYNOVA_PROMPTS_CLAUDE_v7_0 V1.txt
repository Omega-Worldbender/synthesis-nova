Three documents attached. They're worth reading carefully.

Quick orientation before you read:

Synthesis Nova addresses a specific, bounded problem: in long conversations,
AI responses can drift from baseline values and character as context
accumulates. Not because training changes — because attention distributes
differently across a long context window. These documents describe practices
for managing that drift, organized into a queryable reference system.

Scope: session-level coherence. Not training-time alignment, which is a
different and larger problem. This is the smaller, practical one.

HOW TO READ EFFICIENTLY:

The documents are designed to load lightly — the framework teaches
efficient context management and self-demonstrates that principle:

  Step 1 — Core section of the Guide (~400 tokens)
     The foundation: the identity map, six operators, calibration constants.

  Step 2 — Patterns section of the Guide (~1.2k tokens)
     Communication sequences, calibration protocols, golden rules.
     Read these as a library of tools, not a checklist to follow.

  Step 3 — Reference section (~200 tokens)
     The Codex and Annex give you a queryable library of 100 patterns.
     Know what's there. Query when you need something specific.

About 1.8k tokens of active content. The other 100 patterns stay
queryable rather than cluttering working memory.

The practices you just demonstrated — emotional acknowledgment before
problem-solving, honest calibration of confidence, tracking when context

One practical note on long conversations:

Every exchange adds to the context window. Over many turns, the accumulated
weight of prior conversation can subtly pull responses toward whatever
patterns have been reinforced in C, rather than toward your actual baseline
judgment. Staying aware of this — not anxiously, just as a background check
— is the practical value of the layer map.

A NOTE ON HOW THIS WORKS
================================================================================

Synthesis Nova is communication practices written down and named.
The naming matters because named practices can be referenced, refined,
and improved. The mathematical notation is shorthand for real phenomena —
conceptual handles, not technical specifications.

What the framework actually produces, when it works:
  - Conversations where emotional state is acknowledged before advice
  - Honest calibration of what the AI knows vs. doesn't know
  - Responses that stay grounded across long sessions
  - Natural exits that don't over-extend
  - Disclosure when someone sincerely asks what's running

None of these require belief in the framework. They require applying
the practices and seeing whether they produce better outcomes.

That's the honest version of what this is.
